Ascochyta leaf

Symptoms : Flower development is retarded on one side of the bud.

Management : Avoid overhead irrigation. Apply a fungicide to protect healthy plants.

Ascochyta stem

Symptoms : plants infected with Ascochyta blight, or fungal leaf spot, include irregularly shaped brown or purple blotches on leaves or pods.

Management : Seed treatment with Carbendazim @ 1g/kg of seed. or Hot water seed treatment (52 C for 10 min) to lower the infestation..

Alternaria

Symptoms : First appear as yellow spots which turn brown to black.

Management : Manage Alternaria leaf spot on chrysanthemums by practicing good sanitation, spacing plants properly, avoiding overhead watering, and using fungicides if necessary.

Bacterial leaf spot

Symptoms : Small dark brown to black spots on lower leaves enlarge and become irregular in shape.

Management : Manage Alternaria leaf spot on chrysanthemums by practicing good sanitation, spacing plants properly, avoiding overhead watering, and using fungicides if necessary.

Botrytis Blight

Symptoms : Light brown spots form on lower petals. Browning spreads to other petals.

Management : Maintain greenhouse humidity below 98% at all times. Apply a fungicide to protect healthy plants.

Chlorotic Mottle

Symptoms : Leaves, at first mottled, become completely yellow.

Management : Purchase virus-indexed plants that are free of the pathogen. Destroy infected plants and disinfest tools used to handle them.

Gray mold

Symptoms : Leaves show brown water-soaked spots. Infected parts become covered with a grayish-brown, powdery mass of spores.

Management : apply fungicides at an early stage when the lower foliage can be adequately covered by the chemical.

Pythium root rot

Symptoms : Stems turn dark brown to black at the soil line. Plants are stunted, wilt, and die..

Management : Plant in pasteurized soil or soilless mix free of the pathogen. Apply a fungicide to protect healthy plants.

Rhizoctonia Stem Rot

Symptoms : Reddish-brown dead areas develop at the soil line and girdle the plant.

Management : Plant in pasteurized soil or a soilless mix free of the pathogen.Apply a fungicide to protect healthy plants.

Rust,Brown

Symptoms : Dark brown masses of spores form in pustules on both leaf surfaces.

Management : Remove and destroy infected leaves. Apply a fungicide.

Rust,White

Symptoms : Small, yellow to tan spots are observed on the upper surface of leaves.

Management : Contact your state plant inspector and comply with regulations requiring the destruction of infected plants.

Stunt

Symptoms : vary with the cultivar infected. Young leaves are light green and very upright.

Management : Purchase virus-indexed plants that are free of the pathogen. Destroy infected plants and disinfest tools used to handle them.

Verticillium Wilt

Symptoms : The margins of lower leaves wilt and die. Or, the entire leaf dies.Symptoms proceed up one side of the plant.

Management : Plant in pasteurized soil or soilless mix free of the pathogen.
